## Eightfold AI

### Product / Feature Releases
- Eightfold was positioned as a Strategic Leader in the 2026 Fosway 9-Grid for Talent Acquisition for the fourth consecutive year, a ranking that reflects an enterprise shift toward skills intelligence and agentic talent acquisition.
- In mid-May, Eightfold launched "TalentForge," which lets enterprises build custom HR software on top of its platform - a notable expansion from "talent intelligence vendor" toward a platform/extensibility play.
- Per third-party analysis, Eightfold has also rolled out an **AI Interviewer** (autonomous candidate interviewing) and an **Interview Companion** (real-time assistant for human interviewers) - positioning them aggressively in the "agentic interviewing" space.

**So what for Phenom:** TalentForge's "build on our platform" angle could be used in competitive deals where customers want extensibility - worth checking whether Phenom's own extensibility/marketplace story is being told as clearly. The AI Interviewer push also overlaps with any interview-automation roadmap items Phenom has in flight.

### News, Funding & Corporate Moves
- Eightfold announced the winners of its 2026 Pathfinder Awards on June 11, recognizing customers driving transformational change in HR through talent intelligence and agentic AI, with one award category specifically honoring organizations that move beyond basic automation to embrace agentic AI as a "Digital Worker."
- Eightfold remains a Series E company with $410M raised total and a current valuation of about $2.1B.

**So what for Phenom:** The "agentic AI as Digital Worker" framing is becoming a recurring industry narrative (SmartRecruiters and Beamery are using similar language) - worth aligning Phenom's own agent messaging to this emerging shared vocabulary, or differentiating deliberately from it.

### Pricing & Packaging
- No public pricing changes found. Eightfold AI's entry-level pricing is listed as "no pricing available" on G2, consistent with a custom-enterprise-quote model - no change from baseline.

### Reviews & Analyst Sentiment
- On G2, Eightfold AI holds a 4.2/5 rating across 204 reviews, with 74.1% of reviews from enterprise customers. Reviewers rated the two platforms as equally easy to use, but found Eightfold AI easier to set up, while Phenom was preferred on administration and ease of doing business.

### Phenom vs. Eightfold AI (third-party mentions)
- One buyer's guide describes Phenom as "less of an Eightfold competitor and more of a complementary tool," characterizing Phenom as a talent experience platform (TEP) focused on the candidate journey from discovery to onboarding, versus Eightfold's talent-intelligence/matching focus.
- Reviewers felt Phenom Applied AI meets their business needs better than Eightfold AI overall, while rating Eightfold's ongoing product support more favorably.

## SmartRecruiters

### Product / Feature Releases
- SmartRecruiters shipped its June 2026 product release just two days ago, focused on "more control and confidence in hiring workflows" - giving admins more granular control over applicant data transfers (recruiter override permissions, screening-question transfer controls) and improving "Winston Match" to prioritize candidate-verified experience/education data over resume-parsed data.
- Other recent "What's New" items include an AI assistant that explains SmartRecruiters features in natural language, a candidate-facing AI disclosure notice for transparency, and Winston Match adding Swedish-language support.
- In April, SmartRecruiters laid out a broader 2026 vision including agentic interviewing, an agentic CRM, deeper SAP SuccessFactors integration, and a fraud-detection capability for applications.

**So what for Phenom:** SmartRecruiters' release cadence is now genuinely monthly and AI-feature-dense - this is a faster public iteration pace than is typical for enterprise HR tech, likely fueled by SAP's resources post-acquisition. If Phenom's roadmap communications are less frequent/visible externally, that could create a perception gap even if underlying velocity is comparable.

### News, Funding & Corporate Moves
- Following SAP's acquisition of SmartRecruiters (completed September 2025), SmartRecruiters is now integrated with SAP SuccessFactors solutions, and starting in 2026, Winston and SAP's generative AI "Joule" will work together as connected agents - laying the foundation for a unified, AI-enabled talent architecture across the SAP Business Suite.
- SmartRecruiters also reaffirmed a $5 million grant program supporting workforce access and economic mobility (announced Feb 10, 2026).

**So what for Phenom:** This is the single biggest structural story in the competitive set right now. SmartRecruiters is no longer "just an ATS competitor" - it's becoming a packaged entry point into the SAP SuccessFactors ecosystem, with SAP's Joule agent layer behind it. For any deals where the prospect is an SAP HCM customer, SmartRecruiters+SAP may now have a structural bundling advantage that's worth tracking in win/loss data.

### Pricing & Packaging
- SmartRecruiters' Essential plan reportedly starts at $14,995/yr, though third-party integration costs can push total spend past $55K+ in some deployments; the platform has discontinued its free "SmartStart" tier and now requires sales contact for all plans beyond Essential.

### Reviews & Analyst Sentiment
- No new G2/analyst rating changes identified in this window beyond the Fosway/SAP coverage above.

### Phenom vs. SmartRecruiters (third-party mentions)
- One buyer's matrix describes SmartRecruiters as "an enterprise ATS with Winston AI, which handles candidate matching and conversational screening - good for mid-to-large organizations looking for a modern ATS with AI layered on top," distinguishing it from broader talent-experience platforms like Phenom.
- A separate "data-driven recruiting" roundup lists Phenom, Eightfold, Beamery, and SmartRecruiters together as top-five picks, suggesting they're frequently shortlisted against each other in the same enterprise evaluations.
